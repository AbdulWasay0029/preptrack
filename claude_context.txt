What PrepTrack is now
A personal AI coach that prepares Indian CS students for placement interviews.

The old version just sent you 3 LeetCode questions every day. That's useless because you don't know if you're practicing the right things, at the right level, for the right company.

The new version answers the one question every student actually has:

"Am I ready for this interview?"

How it works — 5 layers
Layer 1 — Diagnostic
You open the app, pick your target company (say Amazon), and take a 20-minute test. 5 questions, timed. You type your approach — not actual code, just how you'd solve it.

The AI reads your responses and gives you an honest score. "You are 45% ready for an Amazon SDE1 interview. You're okay on Arrays but weak on Trees and DP."

Now you know where you actually stand. No other product tells you this.

Layer 2 — Pattern Training
Instead of random questions, you learn the 15 patterns that cover 80% of all DSA interview problems.

Things like: Sliding Window, Two Pointers, Tree BFS, Dynamic Programming.

Once you understand a pattern, you can solve ANY problem that uses it — even if you've never seen it before. This is the difference between recalling solutions and actually thinking.

Each pattern has 5 problems in order, easy to hard. You do them in sequence.

Layer 3 — Mock Interview
This is the big one. Nothing like this exists for Indian placement prep.

You click "Start Mock Interview." A timer starts — 45 minutes, like a real interview. A question appears. You type your solution and explain your approach.

Then the AI responds as the interviewer. It asks follow-up questions. "What's the time complexity?" "What if the array was sorted?" "Can you optimize this?" "What about this edge case?"

You go back and forth 3-4 times. At the end you get a full feedback report — was your approach correct, was your communication clear, did you handle edge cases, would they hire you.

This is what you were scared about. Not knowing how an interview feels. Now you practice it before the real thing.

Layer 4 — Real Feedback
After every mock session you get specific feedback. Not just pass/fail.

"Your approach was correct but you didn't mention time complexity until I asked. In real interviews, always state it upfront. Your communication was clear but you hesitated on the edge case."

Actionable. Specific. Like a coach, not a judge.

Layer 5 — Readiness Dashboard
Instead of a streak counter, your dashboard shows a readiness score that updates after every session.

"67% ready for Amazon SDE1. Weak areas: Dynamic Programming, Graphs. At your current pace: ready in 4 weeks."

One clear number. One clear next action. Always know what to do next.

What stays from the old version
Everything you already built. The bot, the questions database, the web dashboard, the backend — all of it stays. We're building on top, not starting over.

The Telegram bot becomes your daily notification — "Hey, you have a mock session scheduled today" or "Here's your pattern question for today."

The old version was worth ₹199/month maybe. Questionable.

This version is a coaching product. Students pay ₹500-1000/month for coaching institutes that give them less than this. A product that tells you honestly if you're ready, trains you on patterns, and simulates real interview pressure — that's worth ₹499/month easily.

What gets built and when
This week — Diagnostic assessment. You take a test, get a score.
Next week — Pattern curriculum. Structured learning, 15 patterns.
Week after — Mock interview. The AI interviewer.
Final week — Readiness dashboard. Everything ties together.
That's it. That's the product.

The core idea: most students fail interviews not because they don't know DSA, but because they've never practiced under pressure, never gotten real feedback, and never known honestly where they stand.

PrepTrack fixes all three.
